BREAKFAST

Ingredients: 3 Slices of Bacon, 2 Eggs, 1 Slice of Bread, 1/2 Apple, 1 Orange, 1/2 Cup Grapes, 1 Tbs Butter, 1/4 Tsp Salt, 1/4 Tsp Pepper

Directions: 1. Cook the bacon in the oven at 410 degrees F for 10-12 mins, depending on your desired crispness. Once done, remove the bacon from the oven and set aside to cool for a later step. 2. Wash your apple and grapes. Slice the apple and set both aside for later. You can choose to cut the grapes in half or leave them whole. 3. Peel your orange and separate the slices, place with the apple and grapes. 4. Whisk the eggs in a bowl and season with salt and pepper. 5. Melt butter into a frying pan, ensuring to coat the sides. Place eggs into the pan and scramble with your spatula until the desired doneness. 6. Wile the eggs are cooking you can toast your slice of bread. Feel free to top your toast with butter, jam, avacado, peanut butter, etc. 7. Remove the eggs from the heat and assemble your plate. You can top your eggs with cheese, salsa, or hot sauce. You can mix up the fruit options, as well as the proteins. Try it with turkey or pork sausage patties or links, or chorizo.




LUNCH

Ingredients: 3 Flour Tortillas, 9 Coconut Shrimp, 1/2 Cup Shredded Cabbage, 1/4 Mango, 1/4 Habanero, 1 Tsp Honey, 1 Lime, 1/4 Tsp Salt, 1/4 Tsp Pepper, 1 Tbs Coconut

Directions: 1. Cook shrimp in the oven according to package instructions. Once done, set aside for later use. 2. Wash the shredded cabage, mango, habanero, and lime. 3. Take your shredded cabbage and toss in a bowl with salt, pepper, and lime juice. Wrap with plastic wrap and place in the fridge until later. 4. Finally chop the habanero and cube the mango. 5. Toss habanero and mango in a bowl with lime juice, lime zest, coconut, and honey. Taste test the mixture to ensure it is to your liking. If you need it sweeter add more honey, need more heat add more habanero. 6. Take the shredded cabbage out of the fridge and toss it together with the mango habanero mixture, and set it back into the fridge. 7. Toast the flour tortillas in a frying pan to your desired crispness and then assemble your tacos. 8. Place 3 coconut shrimp on each tortilla, layer with the desired mango habanero salsa, garnish with cilantro, and enjoy. Feel free to substitue the flour tortillas for corn, both taste amazing.




DINNER

Ingredients: 1/2 Lb Salmon, 4 Pieces of Asparagus, 2 Slices of Bacon, 1 Tbs olive oil, 1/4 Tsp Parsley, 1/4 Tsp Salt, 1/4 Tsp Pepper, 1/2 Tsp Lemon Pepper, 1/2 Tsp Garlic, 1/4 Cup Soy Sauce, 1 Tbs Brown Sugar, 1/4 Red Pepper Flakes, 1 Tbs Shredded Parmesan

Directions: 1. Season the salmon with salt, pepper, lemon pepper, garlic, and parsley and set aside. 2. Clean and snip the ends of the asparagus off. Toss the asparagus in olive oil, salt and pepper. Take your strips of bacon and slice them in half length wise. Take one of the halves and wrap the piece around a stalk of asparagus, and place onto a baking sheet. Repeat with the other 3 pieces. 3. Place your salmon on the grill to cook, skin side down. A Cedar Plank can also be utilized in the grilling process if you so choose. 4. Place your asparagus into the oven to roast at 375 degrees for 15-20 mins, depending on your desired doneness. 5. While your salmon and asparagus are cooking whip up a sauce for your salmon. Mix the soy sauce, brown sugar, and red pepper flakes together. Heat in the microwave for 10-15 seconds, to help evaportate the sugar crystals. 6. Start to assemble your plate with your salmon and aspargus. Drizzle your sauce onto the salmon, and enjoy.
